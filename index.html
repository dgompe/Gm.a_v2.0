01 Executive summary

A platform to end metabolic disease.

589 million adults live with diabetes worldwide and over 4 in 10 do not know it. It silently damages the eye, the kidney and the foot, yet the tests that catch this sit in separate clinics most patients never fully reach.

02 The problem

A silent pandemic, caught too late.

Diabetes damages blood vessels and nerves for years without symptoms. By the time the damage shows, it destroys three organs, each screened in a different clinic that most patients never fully reach.

The eye

Retinopathy → blindness

Retinal screening needs a specialist camera most patients skip until vision is already lost.

The kidney

Nephropathy → dialysis

A simple urine test predicts kidney failure years ahead, yet it is rarely done on schedule.

The foot

Ulcer → amputation

Loss of sensation hides an ulcer until it is limb threatening. This is where our own sensor fits.

07 The model layer

Federated. Private first.

Personalized

Each patient has their own model, learning from their glucose, tests and habits over roughly six months, then refining continuously.

B

Federated

Models train across patients, clinics and partners. Sensitive clinical data is never centralized, which eases privacy and regulation.

C

Aggregate learning improves every individual model. More patients means better predictions for all, without moving the data.

08 Motion system

Moving prevention toward access for all.

A motion system built on a simple truth: health has no boundaries. We grounded it in accessibility, not just in appearance but in behavior, designed to move with intention and reflect Glumia's belief that healthcare should meet people where they are, not where the system expects them to be.

Fluid transitions, dimensional layering, dynamic pacing.

The animation direction breaks from the rigid, one-dimensional approach often seen in the category. Every movement reinforces the idea that health has no boundaries, regardless of shape, size, age or circumstance. The result is a brand that does not just talk about inclusivity, but actively embodies it in every frame.

From that foundation, we mapped the system to three core behaviors, so every interaction, transition and gesture reinforces the emotional clarity and accessibility at the heart of the brand.

Behavior 01

Remove Barriers

Care rarely moves in straight lines. Zooming, layering and revealing become tools that help people discover what is actually possible in healthcare.

Behavior 02

Show, Don't Tell

Each sequence acts as a guide. Elements stack, shift and unfold in rhythm, helping people move through complexity with ease and clarity.

Behavior 03

Human Connection

Movement carries warmth. Gestures reach toward the person, closing distance rather than performing at them, so the system feels human at every step.

12 Regulatory strategy

Cross the line deliberately.

Software becomes a medical device the moment it has a medical purpose, and that includes prevention, prediction and monitoring. We choose exactly when to step across.

Stage 1 · now

Qualified non-device software

Launch keeping the software a non-device, running the insurer pilot and gathering outcome data without medical claims that trigger classification.

Stage 2 · evidence

Pilot + outcome data

400-patient insurer pilot produces real completion rates and complications caught early, building the evidence base a regulator and a payer both need.

Stage 3 · SaMD

Migrate to CE / MDR

Step deliberately into regulated SaMD as claims deepen toward prediction and, eventually, insulin decisions. Defensible and reimbursable.

European Union

Under MDR / IVDR, software with a medical purpose is a device. If AI-based and class IIa+, the EU AI Act adds a second high-risk "dual-compliance" layer.

United States

The FDA took a more deregulatory posture in Jan 2026, broadening exempt "general wellness" and easing clinical-decision-support rules. A friendlier consumer on-ramp.
